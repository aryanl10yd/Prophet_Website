Martin Luther's Public Life

Martin Luther's Beliefs/Religious Views
   Martin Luther King Jr. was a large historical figure, as he was the one who lead the largest black civil rights movement to end the segregation of African Americans in every part of America, spefically the more southern parts. He believed in Christianity, and had a family full of preachers and baptists.
Social/Political views Martin Luther challenged
   Martin Luther was a leader in the black civil rights movement, as he was a social/civil rights activist, and a baptist minister. Because of his actions to take a stand on the original laws of segregation, millions of colored people now hold the liberty of freedom.

Martin Luther's Impact

Why and When did Martin Luther start his journey?
   Having been born in the year 1929, on the month of January 15th, in Atlanta, Georgia, Martin Luther King Jr. started his journey at an early age of 15, after becoming determined to end racial segregation when his white childhood friend told him they couldn't be friends anymore since his parents had put him into a segregated school. After having graduated from college and becoming a baptist minister, taking after both his , who had college educations. His father would later take over his father-in-law's position as a pastor of their church, Ebenezer Baptist, in Atlanta. he would often hold many congregations there, and would eventually lead nonviolent civil rights protests and movements.
What Impact did Martin Luther have?
   Through his nonviolent civil rights movements and protests, he was able to help create new laws and acts for the racial discrimination and segregation that was occuring. Today, millions of people are free from racial discrimination and are free in democracy and have their own civil rights. Martin Luther also helped to stop not only racial discrimination, but also gender, color, and national origin discrimination as well. Even if he wasn't considered a prophet through the church, he was truly one at heart, and was one to millions of people both back then and today. His actions of leading a nonviolent civil rights movement resulted in allowing for larger democracy for everyone of all races and civil rights for everyone, no matter their gender, race, color, or nationality.
